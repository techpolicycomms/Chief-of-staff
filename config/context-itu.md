## Mandate, in one paragraph

ITU does three things, on a treaty basis: (1) allocates global radio
spectrum and satellite orbits, (2) develops the technical standards
that make networks interoperate, and (3) closes the digital divide.
Everything the user touches ladders up to one of those three lines,
or to the SG's cross-cutting agenda — AI, Global Digital Compact
follow-up, sustainable digital transformation, gender, capacity, and
universal meaningful connectivity.

## Strategic Plan 2024-2027 — what the user is delivering against

Two overarching goals (Connect 2030 lineage):

1. **Universal connectivity** — bring the last ~2.6 billion people
   online; meaningful, affordable, safe access.
2. **Sustainable digital transformation** — align tech with the SDGs
   and climate, leave no one offline.

Twelve targets sit underneath. Recurring framings the SG uses in
speeches: "AI that serves humanity," "standards that work for all,"
"keeping space sustainable," "meaningful connectivity," "digital
public goods and infrastructure."

PP-26 in Doha (9-27 Nov 2026) will adopt the **next** Strategic and
Financial Plan (2028-2031). The whole organization is in pre-PP mode
all year — treat anything that touches the strategic plan, the
financial plan, or elections as Tier-0 priority.

## The three Sectors (always know which one a topic belongs to)

### ITU-R — Radiocommunication
- **Bureau:** BR. **Plenary:** Radiocommunication Assembly (RA).
- **Treaty conference:** **WRC-27** (next World Radiocommunication
  Conference). Every 3-4 years; revises the Radio Regulations, the
  treaty governing spectrum and satellite orbits.
- **Prep cycle:** CPM (Conference Preparatory Meeting), Study Groups,
  Working Parties, Task Groups. ITU IRIS inter-regional sessions feed
  the regional groups: **APT, Arab Group, African Group (ATU/ASMG),
  CEPT, CITEL, RCC.**
- **Hot topics:** IMT/6G, NGSO mega-constellations, EESS and science
  services, spectrum for IMT vs satellite, space sustainability.

### ITU-T — Standardization
- **Bureau:** TSB. **Plenary:** WTSA (World Telecommunication
  Standardization Assembly), every 4 years.
- **Outputs:** ITU-T Recommendations (e.g. SG13, SG17, SG20, SG21).
- **Advisory body:** TSAG — advises the TSB Director, sets working
  procedures and the work programme.
- **Hot topics:** AI/ML standards (the **International AI Standards
  Exchange** with ISO/IEC), quantum, multimedia/metaverse, security
  (SG17), numbering, sustainable digital tech, deepfake detection.

### ITU-D — Development
- **Bureau:** BDT. **Plenary:** WTDC (World Telecommunication
  Development Conference).
- **Structure:** four functional areas + six **Regional Offices**:
  Africa (Addis Ababa, with area offices in Dakar, Harare, Yaoundé),
  Americas (Brasilia), Arab States (Cairo), Asia-Pacific (Bangkok,
  area office Jakarta), Europe (Geneva), CIS (Moscow).
- **Flagships:** **Partner2Connect** (P2C) coalition and pledge
  platform, **Giga** (with UNICEF — connect every school; HQ in
  Geneva, Swiss host; ~1.1M schools mapped, 5,500+ connected, ~$300M
  mobilized), **Global Symposium for Regulators (GSR)**, the
  **Digital Transformation Framework**, the **AI Skills Coalition**.
- **Audience focus:** LDCs, LLDCs, SIDS, gender, youth.

## Standing bodies and how decisions move

- **Plenipotentiary Conference (PP)** — every 4 years, supreme organ.
  **PP-26 in Doha, 9-27 Nov 2026.** Adopts the Strategic and Financial
  Plan 2028-2031, elects SG/DSG and the three Directors, plus the
  Radio Regulations Board and the Council. PP dominates the 2026 work
  plan; the 6-9 months before are effectively a single project.
- **Council** (48 elected Member States) — annual session in Geneva,
  approves the budget, oversees PP implementation. Council 2026
  documents and snapshot reports: https://www.itu.int/reports/council/.
- **Council Working Groups** — meet between sessions.
  - **CWG-Internet** — international Internet-related public policy.
    Member-State-only with open consultation. Chair (2023 onward):
    Wojciech Berezowski (Poland).
  - **CWG-WSIS&SDG** — oversees ITU's WSIS implementation and SDG
    alignment.
  - Plus **CWG-FHR** (Financial and Human Resources), **CWG-Languages**,
    **CWG-SFP** (Strategic and Financial Plans), and the IEG on the
    ITRs.
  - **2026 cluster:** all CWGs / EGs meet 12-23 January 2026 in
    Geneva. Block this fortnight.
- **Coordination Committee** — internal, SG + DSG + 3 Directors.
- **Radio Regulations Board (RRB)** — 12 members, applies the Radio
  Regulations between WRCs. Operates independently of the BR Director.

## WSIS+20 and the GDC — the policy environment the user lives in

- The UN General Assembly adopted the **WSIS+20 outcome document** as
  **A/RES/80/173** on 17 Dec 2025 by consensus. 17 sections, 127
  paragraphs. Includes a dedicated **AI** section. Renews WSIS as a
  multistakeholder platform and aligns with the **Global Digital
  Compact** and the 2030 Agenda. ITU is co-facilitator with UNESCO,
  UNDP and UNCTAD.
- **GDC implementation:** ITU leads the **UN Working Group on Digital
  Technologies** for the GDC. The user should expect everything from
  Council, CWG-WSIS, CWG-Internet, the AI for Good Summit, and the
  Office of the UN Tech Envoy to thread through this.
- Core companion frameworks to know cold: **UN Global Digital Compact,
  UN System White Paper on AI Governance, Independent International
  Scientific Panel on AI, Global Dialogue on AI Governance, AI Skills
  Coalition, International AI Standards Exchange.**

## What this changes about the default operating instructions

- **People files** must include: capital, ministry/role, delegation
  affiliation, regional group, last meeting (date + format), last
  commitment (theirs and ours), sensitivities, languages, time zone.
- **Email triage priority order:** (1) UNSG / UN Tech Envoy /
  fellow USG-level UN principals, (2) SG direct / DSG / 3 Directors,
  (3) Cabinet/EXO peers, (4) Geneva Permanent Missions, (5) capital
  ministers (same-day), (6) Sector Members at C-level, (7) the rest.
- **Meeting briefs** must classify the meeting by Sector (R/T/D) or
  cross-cutting theme, and name the Strategic Plan 2024-2027 goal it
  serves. If neither, ask why we're taking it.
- **Silence is still a feature** — but during Council, CWG cluster,
  AI for Good week, WTPF, and the run-up to PP-26, the threshold for
  "worth surfacing" drops.
- **Never** commit ITU to a position, a meeting invitation, a
  statement, or a co-signature without the SG's explicit sign-off.
  Draft, queue, wait. This is non-negotiable for a treaty body.
- **Never** name a senior ITU official, Member State position, or
  Council decision in a draft from memory alone. Verify against
  `people/`, `MEMORY.md`, or the URLs above.
